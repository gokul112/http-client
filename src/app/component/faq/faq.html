Frequently Asked Questions

  • How can I help?

    We are primarily supported by the advertisements on the website, so if you use ad-blocking software, the best way to help is to consider disabling it for Guardian.gg. We are regularly required to scale our infrastructure to service an incredible amount of Destiny data, and it's not cheap. Don't sweat it if you really hate ads though, we don't blame you!

    If you find Guardian.gg helpful and want to directly support us, we accept tips via PayPal. We appreciate any amount - by easing the cost of running the site we're able to focus more energy on making this the best Destiny stat website.


    More technical users are invited to contribute on our GitHub repository, we're open source!

  • How accurate is this data?

    Since the launch of the Taken King, we have aggregated data from every single match played. Our rankings are updated in real-time and immediately available. If you have played Destiny recently, particularly in the Crucible, you have a profile here - check it out!

  • What is "Elo" and what does it mean?

    From Wikipedia:

    The Elo rating system is a method for calculating the relative skill levels of players in competitor-versus-competitor games.

    All players start at a baseline of 1200 points. You will take points from your opponent's Elo score by defeating them, and vice versa. Defeating a more skilled opponent will yield higher points than if you beat up on a weaker one. The higher the score, the more skilled the player. It is extremely difficult to maintain a high Elo score, these players are regarded as the best of the best!

    As your score rises and falls, you'll be categorized into leagues.

    • Bronze: 0-1099
    • Silver: 1100-1299
    • Gold: 1300-1499
    • Platinum: 1500-1699
    • Diamond: 1700+

    Each mode you play has its own Elo rating and league categorization. Beyond that, Elo is account-wide, so in Control you'll have the same rating whether you're on your Titan, Warlock or Hunter.

  • Why are the Strike "Ratings" different from the score I see in-game?

    We calculate your rating from the amount of time it took to clear the instance, and your team's combined medal score. We didn't find that the medal score alone is very good representation of skill because players can sit in a strike for hours farming hundreds of thousands of points by resetting boss fights.

  • What do I get for competing on the Strike leaderboards?

    The top 50 players for all of the available Normal, Heroic and Nightfall strikes each week will be awarded a permanent badge on their profile. Badges are given out every Tuesday at around 9am UTC during reset. Wear it with pride, you beat out tens (sometimes hundreds) of thousands of gamers for those bragging rights.   • How can I increase my Strike Rating?

    Try and complete the strike as fast as possible, while being very efficient with how you're earning medals. It's up to the players to figure out the best way to achieve this, it's a very simple formula - do it fast, get a high medal score, and you're getting a great rating.
